Introduction to Korean Cuisine

Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors and the harmony of various ingredients. Known for its use of garlic, ginger, and red pepper, Korean food emphasizes balance and nutrition. The cuisine often incorporates fermented foods like kimchi, which provide a unique taste and are beneficial for digestion.

Popular Korean Dishes

Click on a dish to see more information.

  • Bibimbap

    A mixed rice dish with vegetables, meat, and a spicy gochujang sauce.

  • Kimchi

    Fermented cabbage seasoned with red pepper, garlic, and ginger.

  • Bulgogi

    Marinated beef grilled with garlic and sesame oil for a savory taste.

  • Tteokbokki

    Spicy rice cakes cooked in a hot pepper sauce with vegetables.

  • Galbitang

    Beef short rib soup with radish and garlic, known for its nourishing flavor.

  • Doenjang Jjigae

    Savory soybean paste stew with tofu and veggies, a Korean staple.

  • Samgyeopsal

    Grilled pork belly with garlic and chili, enjoyed in lettuce wraps.

  • Japchae

    Stir-fried glass noodles with veggies, sweet and savory, perfect for celebrations.

Flavors and Ingredients

Korean cuisine is characterized by its use of contrasting flavors - spicy, sour, sweet, and savory. Key ingredients include soy sauce, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, and gochujang (red pepper paste). Fresh vegetables and rice are also fundamental to Korean dishes, creating a perfect balance of taste and nutrition.

Explore Korean Cuisine through These TV Programs!


Hansik Contest

The show features 10 teams, each representing a region of South Korea, competing in Korean cuisine. Teams often pair a master chef with a less experienced chef, highlighting regional diversity and culinary skills, with one team eliminated each week.

Black and White Chef

A survival program in which a total of 100 chefs, ranging from veteran chefs to star chefs whose faces are already known to the public, compete only for taste.
